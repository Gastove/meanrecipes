<h3 class="post-title">
I'm feeling daring
</h3>

<div class="post-body">
<div>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>I have finally learned how to use the espresso machine that Brandon chased down on eBay and gave me for Christmas in 2011! &nbsp;The best part of this development, however, is not the double espresso that I can now enjoy each morning while sitting on the living room floor with June, reading <i><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Madeline-Ludwig-Bemelmans/dp/0670014079/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;keywords=madeline%20board%20book&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;qid=1367640919&amp;s=books&amp;sr=1-1&amp;tag=orangette-20" target="_blank">Madeline</a></i>&nbsp;or singing along (poorly, loudly) to our favorite song, <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1TD_pSeNelU" target="_blank">On the Road Again</a>. No, no, the best part is that while I make said double espresso, I get to recite aloud for June and <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/molly_orangette/sets/72157627711600121/" target="_blank">Alice</a>, in my best/worst Italian accent, the&nbsp;<i>molto</i>&nbsp;gag-worthy slogan written in loopy script on the side of the machine:<br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: center;">
<i>For Music ~ Puccini</i></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<i>For Art ~ Bernini&nbsp;</i></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<i>For&nbsp;</i><i>Espresso ~ Pasquini</i></div>
<br />
In other news, do you know what goes nicely with espresso? Cream cheese pound cake. CREAM CHEESE POUND CAKE! (Holy holy holy, <a href="http://orangette.blogspot.com/2013/04/april-7.html" target="_blank"><i>finally</i></a>. <a href="http://www.delanceyseattle.com/" target="_blank">Delancey</a> and <a href="http://essexbarseattle.com/" target="_blank">Essex</a> have been needy lately, heedlessly gobbling up my time. Stupid restaurants. My sincerest apologies.)<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="MsoNormal">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-sAc0ax3sSX0/UXinj1V1sUI/AAAAAAAAEnU/Ef06cRl8o5Q/s1600/pound+cake.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="550" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-sAc0ax3sSX0/UXinj1V1sUI/AAAAAAAAEnU/Ef06cRl8o5Q/pound+cake.jpg" width="550" /></a></div>
<b><br /></b>Listen: Im not normally pound cake person. &nbsp;Not a <i>real</i>&nbsp;pound cake person. I may have gotten riled up about a <a href="http://orangette.blogspot.com/2012/04/no-such-thing.html" target="_blank">pistachio pound cake</a>&nbsp;last year, and there was that&nbsp;<a href="http://orangette.blogspot.com/2009/03/i-mean-it.html" target="_blank">sweet potato pound cake</a> a few years ago, and I may have put a berry pound cake recipe in my first book, and I may have even found my way around a few&nbsp;<a href="https://www.google.com/search?q=sara+lee+pound+cake&amp;safe=off&amp;tbm=isch&amp;tbo=u&amp;source=univ&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=1ut5UaeGCun6iwKP44H4DA&amp;ved=0CE0QsAQ&amp;biw=1430&amp;bih=806" target="_blank">Sara Lee frozen pound cakes</a> as a teenager - remember the crust on top? The way it was soft and spongy and eerily uniform in its brownness? I <i>loved</i> that part - but those were all special cases. I dont get wildly excited about pound cake as a general concept. &nbsp;I can get behind a nice, plain cake, maybe&nbsp;<a href="http://orangette.blogspot.com/2008/02/like-lullaby.html" target="_blank">a busy-day cake</a>, but pound cakes are often&nbsp;too&nbsp;plain, too heavy, too doorstoppy. &nbsp;Pound cake, in the classic sense, strikes me mostly as a vehicle for transporting strawberries (or other fruits) and whipped cream from a plate into my mouth. I know that, in the eyes of many, theres all kinds of sacrilege in this paragraph, but Im feeling daring.<br />
<br />
All that said, this pound cake is exceptional. It caught me off guard. I was looking for a way to use up some cream cheese that I had lying around, and I came upon the recipe in the excellent book <i><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Southern-Cakes-Irresistible-Everyday-Celebrations/dp/0811853705/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;tag=orangette-20" target="_blank">Southern Cakes</a></i>, by Nancie McDermott. Thats the same&nbsp;book that gave us the sweet potato pound cake, so its not surprising that this cream cheese version is spot-on. But really, its a keeper. <i>Lovely</i>&nbsp;is the right word for it. Theres nothing revolutionary about the ingredients - just your basic pound cake building blocks, plus a pack of cream cheese - but its unusually moist and even-crumbed, with a top crust that crackles like a wafer.&nbsp;And as you begin to chew, here comes the cream cheese, a gentle tang kicking through the sweetness. I love the way McDermott describes it: she says that the cream cheese makes a&nbsp;quiet little sensation. Am I alone in being unable to use the word <i>sensation</i>&nbsp;without thinking of <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6jujG5X9iZs" target="_blank">INXS</a>, and then having to listen to <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dMi5KU0UjYE" target="_blank">this song</a>&nbsp;a few times, feeling mopey about Michael Hutchences untimely death&nbsp;16 years ago? Probably?<br />
<br />
Anyway, I baked two loaves and froze one of them, and both Brandon and I noticed that the frozen cake, once thawed, was even better than the fresh one had been. This discovery makes me want to bake a half-dozen of these cakes and stash them away for future occasions that demand sweets on short notice. Like tomorrows breakfast.<br />
<br />
<b><br /></b>
<b>Cream Cheese Pound Cake</b><br />
Adapted from <i><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Southern-Cakes-Irresistible-Everyday-Celebrations/dp/0811853705/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;tag=orangette-20" target="_blank">Southern Cakes</a></i>, by Nancie McDermott<br />
<br />
This recipe was shared with McDermott by one Suzanne OHara of Burlington, North Carolina, and it comes together with remarkable speed and ease. I think Ill be making it often.<br />
<br />
3 cups (420 g) all-purpose flour<br />
1 teaspoon baking powder<br />
teaspoon fine salt<br />
2 sticks (226 g) unsalted butter, at room temperature<br />
One 8-ounce (226 g) package cream cheese, at room temperature<br />
3 cups (600 g) sugar<br />
6 large eggs<br />
1 teaspoon vanilla extract<br />
<br />
Preheat the oven to 325F. Grease and flour a 10-inch tube pan or two 9-by-5-inch loaf pans. (I also lined my pans with parchment, because it makes the cakes so easy to remove.)<br />
<br />
In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder,
and salt.<br />
<br />
In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle
attachment, combine the butter and cream cheese, and beat on medium speed until
soft and fluffy. Add the sugar, and continue to beat for about 2 minutes more, stopping once to scrape down the
sides. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Beat in the vanilla. Reduce the mixer speed to low,
and add the flour mixture in three doses, beating only until the flour is absorbed and scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed. Scrape the batter into the prepared pan or pans.<br />
<br />
Bake for about 1 hour and 15 minutes (for a tube pan) or 55 to 60 minutes (for loaf pans), or until the cake is golden brown, pulling away
from the sides of the pan, and a cake tester inserted into the center comes out
clean. Transfer the cake to a wire rack, and cool completely before loosening the sides with a thin knife and removing
the cake from the pan.</div>
